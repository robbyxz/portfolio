CASE STUDY: FRAUD DETECTION SYSTEM

I. Executive Summary & Business Context

Credit card companies must detect fraudulent transactions in real time to minimize financial losses. This project developed predictive models to distinguish between legitimate and fraudulent activity.

II. Data Overview & Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

  • Dataset: Simulated transactions (2019–2020) covering 1,000 customers.
  • Class Imbalance: The data is highly imbalanced, with only 0.58% of transactions being fraudulent.
  • Key Insight: Fraudulent transactions appear more frequently in older age groups (55+) and exhibit specific spending patterns.

III. Methodology & Technical Approach

Data Processing: Features were engineered from raw transaction dates and timestamps; categorical variables were encoded using dummy variables. SMOTE was applied to balance the training data classes.

Model Architecture:
1. Logistic Regression: Served as the baseline model.
2. Random Forest: Utilized to capture complex non-linear patterns.
3. XGBoost: Gradient boosting approach for high-performance classification.

IV. Results & Model Comparison

  • Logistic Regression: ROC-AUC of 0.84 (High false positive rate).
  • Random Forest: ROC-AUC of 0.99 (High precision and recall).
  • XGBoost: ROC-AUC of 0.992 (Most stable performance).

V. Recruiter Focus: Skills & Impact

Tech Stack: Python, Scikit-Learn, XGBoost, Seaborn.

Business Value: Reduces direct financial loss, optimizes operational costs by lowering false alarms for investigation teams, and enhances customer trust.
