CASE STUDY: FRAUD DETECTION SYSTEM

I. Executive Summary & Business Context

Financial institutions face the ongoing challenge of identifying fraudulent transactions instantaneously to prevent direct financial losses. This project focused on building a robust classification pipeline capable of handling highly imbalanced datasets, where fraud represents only a tiny fraction of total transaction volume. The primary objective was to maximize fraud detection (recall) while minimizing the operational burden of false alarms (precision).

II. Data Overview & Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

  • Dataset: A simulated credit card dataset spanning January 1, 2019, to December 31, 2020, covering 1,000 customers and 800 merchants.
  • Class Imbalance: The dataset was heavily imbalanced; non-fraudulent transactions accounted for 99.42% of the volume, with fraudulent transactions making up only 0.58%.
  • Key Insight: Most legitimate transactions were under $200. Conversely, fraudulent transactions exhibited two distinct spending peaks—one around $50 and a secondary peak near $300.

III. Methodology & Technical Approach

The pipeline was built to transform raw transaction logs into actionable predictive signals:

  • Feature Engineering: Raw timestamps were processed into meaningful temporal features (hour, day of the week, month), and customer age was derived from their date of birth. Categorical variables were converted using dummy encoding.
  • Addressing Imbalance: To prevent model bias toward the majority class, I applied the SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Oversampling Technique) on the training set to create synthetic examples of fraudulent transactions.
  • Model Architecture:
    1. Logistic Regression: Established a performance baseline.
    2. Random Forest: Utilized for non-linear pattern identification.
    3. XGBoost: Implemented for high-performance classification and stable ROC-AUC results.

IV. Results & Model Comparison

  • Logistic Regression: ROC-AUC of 0.84.
  • Random Forest: ROC-AUC of 0.99.
  • XGBoost: ROC-AUC of 0.992 (Top performing model for precision-recall balance).

V. Business Impact & Value Proposition

This project moves beyond academic metrics to solve specific operational challenges:

  • Risk Mitigation: High recall proactively identifies fraudulent attempts, directly lowering financial exposure.
  • Operational Cost Reduction: High precision reduces the volume of false-positive alerts, lowering the workload for investigation teams.
  • Customer Experience: Fewer incorrectly blocked legitimate transactions lead to higher customer trust and lower churn.
