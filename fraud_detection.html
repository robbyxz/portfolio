CASE STUDY: FRAUD DETECTION SYSTEM

I. Executive Summary & Business Context

Credit card companies must detect fraudulent transactions in real time to minimize financial losses. This project developed predictive models to distinguish between legitimate and fraudulent activity, focusing on robust detection in highly imbalanced datasets.

II. Data Overview & Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

  • Dataset: Simulated transactions (2019–2020) with over 1,000 customers.
  • Class Imbalance: The dataset is heavily imbalanced, with only 0.58% of transactions labeled as fraud.
  • Key Insight: Transaction patterns showed distinct anomalies in specific demographics and spending thresholds.

III. Methodology & Technical Approach

Data Processing: Cleaned high-volume datasets by removing duplicates. Engineered temporal features (hour, day, month) and calculated customer age to capture fraud correlations. Applied dummy encoding for categorical variables.

Addressing Imbalance: Used SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Oversampling Technique) on the training set to prevent model bias toward the legitimate transaction majority.

Model Architecture:
1. Logistic Regression: Established a baseline performance benchmark.
2. Random Forest: Utilized for complex non-linear pattern identification.
3. XGBoost: Implemented for high-performance classification and stable ROC-AUC results.

IV. Results & Model Comparison

  • Logistic Regression: ROC-AUC of 0.84.
  • Random Forest: ROC-AUC of 0.99.
  • XGBoost: ROC-AUC of 0.992 (Top performing model for precision-recall balance).

V. Recruiter Focus: Skills & Impact

Tech Stack: Python, Pandas, Scikit-Learn, SMOTE, XGBoost.

Business Value: Directly mitigates financial fraud, reduces manual investigation workload via high-precision alerts, and enhances overall customer security.
