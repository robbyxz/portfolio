CASE STUDY: SPAM EMAIL DETECTION & CLASSIFICATION

I. Executive Summary & Business Context

Spam messages expose users to phishing and fraud risks. This project focuses on building an automated email and SMS filtering pipeline to accurately classify messages as either legitimate ("Ham") or "Spam", balancing high recall with precision.

II. Methodology & Technical Approach

  • Preprocessing: Text cleaning, tokenization, and sequence padding.
  • Modeling: Benchmarked performance using Multinomial Naive Bayes, Bernoulli Naive Bayes, and a Deep Learning Recurrent Neural Network (RNN).
  • Optimization: Used Embedding layers and Dropout regularization to prevent overfitting.

III. Business Impact

This automated system reduces phishing exposure and lowers operational costs associated with manual content moderation while maintaining high precision for legitimate business communications.

CASE STUDY: FRAUD DETECTION SYSTEM

I. Executive Summary & Business Context

Financial institutions require robust detection systems to minimize losses from fraudulent credit card transactions. This project developed an automated classification pipeline to distinguish between legitimate and fraudulent activity, focusing on high-performance detection within highly imbalanced datasets.

II. Methodology & Technical Approach

  • Preprocessing: Data cleaning, duplicate removal, and categorical encoding via dummy variables.
  • Feature Engineering: Extracted temporal patterns (hour, day, month) and demographic features (age) to identify risk-prone behavior.
  • Optimization: Applied SMOTE to handle class imbalance (0.58% fraud rate) and benchmarked Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost models.

III. Business Impact

This automated system provides real-time transaction scoring, reducing financial exposure through high recall and minimizing operational costs by lowering false alarms via high-precision classification.
