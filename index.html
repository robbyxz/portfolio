CASE STUDY: SPAM EMAIL DETECTION & CLASSIFICATION

I. Executive Summary & Business Context

Spam messages expose users to phishing and fraud risks. This project focuses on building an automated email and SMS filtering pipeline to accurately classify messages as either legitimate ("Ham") or "Spam", balancing high recall with precision.

II. Methodology & Technical Approach

  • Preprocessing: Text cleaning, tokenization, and sequence padding.
  • Modeling: Benchmarked performance using Multinomial Naive Bayes, Bernoulli Naive Bayes, and a Deep Learning Recurrent Neural Network (RNN).
  • Optimization: Used Embedding layers and Dropout regularization to prevent overfitting.

III. Business Impact

This automated system reduces phishing exposure and lowers operational costs associated with manual content moderation while maintaining high precision for legitimate business communications.
